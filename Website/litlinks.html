General Literature Resources

Voice of the Shuttle: A Web Site for Humanities Research

(VoS is woven by Alan Liu and a development team in the Univeristy of California, Santa Barbara, English Department.)

A great resource for students of literature, as well as for those doing work in other disciplines.

Literary History.com: Indexing the Internet

(Copyright © 1998-2004 by Jan Pridmore.)

Links to articles on particular authors and genres, from the nineteenth and twentieth centuries. The "20th Century Poetry" section provides a good overview of major authors and schools of poetry (borrowed, as the web-author notes, from David Perkins's A History of Modern Poetry.)

Literary Resources on the Net

(This page is maintained by Jack Lynch.)

Created by Jack Lynch, professor of English at Rutgers University, Newark campus. As he puts it, "this set of pages is a collection of links to sites on the Internet dealing especially with English and American literature, excluding most single electronic texts." Although much of the information here is for academics, students of all kinds will find much of interest.

Perseus Digital Library

(© copyright 1997 Trustees of Tufts College. Ed. by Gregory R. Crane.)

The Perseus Web, originating from Tufts University, is a great resource for Ancient Greek and Roman materials, including texts, secondary sources, and images. The site also includes substantial collections of works related to the English Renaissance, the history of London, and the history of California.

Electronic Text Center

(© 2002 The Rector and Visitors of the University of Virginia. Please see Conditions of Use for detailed copyright information.)

Click on "Collections" for an enormous selection of publicly-accessible ebooks, texts, and images. Texts available in almost all major languages. [NB: some resources are restricted to UVA students and faculty.]

The Victorian Web: Literature, History, & Culture in the Age of Victoria

(The Victorian Web is a project funded by the University Scholars Program, National University of Singapore.)

The subtitle says it all: quick overviews of all things Victorian.

WriteNet

(©2005 Teachers & Writers Collaborative)

Sections include: "Poets on Poetry," "Fiction Writers on Fiction Writing," and "Writers on Teaching."

Small Press Distribution


"Small Press Distribution is a non-profit (501(c)3) literary arts organization located in Berkeley, California. Our mission is to nurture a cultural context in which the literary arts are valued and sustained, and we accomplish this mission by providing wholesaling services to independent presses and through our public programs and advocacy efforts. We believe that the written word, in its most eloquent form, deserves the broadest possible distribution.

Founded in 1969, we are the only wholesaler in the country dedicated exclusively to independently published literature. SPD gives the public access to the broad spectrum of literature published today by distributing books to bookstores, libraries, and readers. We take risks on exciting new writers, enabling their work to develop an audience and gain recognition in the marketplace."

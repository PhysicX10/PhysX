Art and Art History Links

The Artchive

(Created by Mark Harden)

Mark Harden's Artchive contains over 2,000 high-quality scans of work from over 200 artists. He includes short biographies for each artist and a small selection of art criticism.

Web Gallery of Art

(© Web Gallery of Art, created by Emil Kren and Daniel Marx.)

"The Web Gallery of Art is a virtual museum and searchable database of European painting and sculpture of the Gothic, Renaissance and Baroque periods (1150-1800), currently containing over 12,100 reproductions. Commentaries on pictures, biographies of artists are available."

Art History Resources on the Web

(Maintained by Chris Witcombe, Professor of Art History, Sweet Briar College, Virginia)

Links to web-based art history resources, from prehistoric to contempory, and to museum websites around the world.

Dia Art Foundation

(Dia Art Foundation, founded in 1974, is a nonprofit institution that plays a vital role among visual arts organizations nationally and internationally by initiating, supporting, presenting, and preserving art projects, and by serving as a locus for interdisciplinary art and criticism.)

In addition to exhibition catalogues, artist web projects, and links to their bookshop, see Dia's poetry section (under "Programs" in the "Chelsea" heading) that features texts (and some audio recordings) by poets who have read at the center, including John Ashberry, Wole Soyinka, Sharon Olds, Galway Kinnell, Quincy Troupe, and many others.

Museum of Modern Art

(© Copyright 2004 The Museum of Modern Art; site designed by Allegra Burnette)

Includes highlights from the collection, discussions of current and past exhibitions, and the "Red Studio" site, designed by and for high school students.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art

(© 2000Ð2004 The Metropolitan Museum of Art. All rights reserved.)

Features works from the permanent collection and new exhibitions. See their "Timeline of Art History."

San Francisco Museum of Modern Art

(Copyright © 2004 San Francisco Museum of Modern Art)

Art from their collections and exhibitions. See also their "E-Space" for web-based artist projects and curatorial experiments.

Tate Online: British and international modern art

(© copyright 2003 Tate, all rights reserved )

Tate holds the national collections of British Art and of international modern art. Sites include: Tate Britain--British Art, 1500-2004; Tate Modern--modern and contemporary art; Net Art--art works commisioned to experience online.

Poetry Links

Poets.org: Academy of American Poets

(Copyright © 1997-2004 by The Academy of American Poets)

Features poets from America and the world over, and includes poems, biographies, audio clips, and links. This site also hosts the National Poetry Calendar.

Electronic Poetry Center
(The EPC is © copyright Loss Peque–o Glazier and Charles Bernstein. The site is hosted by SUNY Buffalo.)

Links to an array of 20th-century and contemporary poets' works, biographies, websites, and soundfiles of readings. Also includes a long list of links to other poetry sites, and information about SUNY Buffalo's renowned Poetics Program.

Cave Canem: A Home for Black Poetry
(Founded in 1996 by poets Cornelius Eady and Toi Derricotte and based in NYC, Cave Canem is a non-profit poetry organization "committed to the discovery and cultivation of new voices in African American poetry.")

Cave Canem organizes workshops, retreats, and a reading series. The website includes an extensive "Featured Poems" section, a calendar of readings, and a long list of links to the work of individual poets.

Favorite Poem Project
(Founded by former U.S. Poet Laureate Robert Pinsky, in partnership with Boston University, the Poetry Society of America, and the Library of Congress.)

"A collection of 50 short video documentaries that showcase individual Americans reading and speaking personally about poems they love."

UbuWeb
(Copyleft 1996-2004. All materials on UbuWeb are being made available for noncommercial and educational use only. All rights belong to the author(s). UbuWeb is completely free.)

Ah, UbuWeb; Wah, UbuWeb? A site dedicated to poetry that is avant-garde, conceptual, found, or otherwise unclassifiable. Also includes critical commentaries from the poets and artists themselves, as well as from philosophers, art historians, and literary critics.

Poetry in Motion Project: Poems on Buses and Subways
(© Poetry Society of America adn MTA/New York City Transit Authority)

Read all the poems presented on NYC buses and subways since 1992. The "Atlas" link lets you see what poems appeared on other mass transit systems, from LA to Chicago to Dallas. Now why did LA get Harryette Mullen's "Wipe That Smile Off Your Aphasia" and not us?

WriteNet
(©2005 Teachers & Writers Collaborative)

Sections include: "Poets on Poetry," "Fiction Writers on Fiction Writing," and "Writers on Teaching." In the "Poets on Poetry" section, "Daniel Kane and others interview poets about their poems, their poetics, and their ideas on how to teach poetry to students in grades K-12." Includes interviews with Harryette Mullen, Robert Pinsky, John Yau, Eileen Myles, and others.

Poets House
(Founded by poet Stanley Kunitz and arts adminstrator Elizabeth Kray.)

Located at 72 Spring Street in Soho, Poets House has been a meeting place for poets and readers since 1985. Home to a fantastic series of readings, lectures, and panel discussions, and to a large poetry library, now searchable online. In addition to their online poetry database, the website includes descriptions of programs and an extensive set of poetry links.

Poetry: The Library of Congress Website

The Library of Congress Poetry site includes a poetry news and events calendar, "A Poem a Day" chosen by former Poet Laureate Billy Collins, a listing of poetry prizes and fellowships, information on the current Poet Laureate and Laureates of the past, and links to the LC poetry and literature archive. Check out the select "Poetry Webcasts"--extended video clips of readings and discussions with poets--from the "Poet Vision" and "Poet and the Poem" series. Poets include Rita Dove, Allen Ginsberg, Denise Levertov, Stanley Kunitz, Billy Collins, Lousie Gluck, and Lucille Clifton.

The Poetry Project at St. Mark's Church

With three reading/performance series a week, workshops, a journal, and archives, The Poetry Project at St. Mark's remains a major center force in the poetry world. Founded in 1966, St. Mark's has hosted readings by, among others, Allen Ginsberg, John Ashbery, Adrienne Rich, Alice Walker, John Cage, Sam Shepard, and Amiri Baraka (Leroi Jones).

The website includes a calendar of events, an e-journal, "Poets and Poems," and a newsletter.

Nuyorican Poets Cafe

"A non-profit organization, the Nuyorican Poets Cafe has emerged as one of the country's most highly respected arts organizations and has become an acclaimed forum for innovative poetry, music, hip hop, video, visual arts, comedy and theatre. Founded circa 1973, The Nuyorican Poets Cafe began as a living room salon in the East Village apartment of writer and poet, Miguel Algarin. Algarin, a college professor at the center of this blossoming arts community, was dedicated to bringing new work into the public eye."

The website is primarily an online calendar of events at the cafe.

Poetry Archives
(Copyright © 1995 - 2003 All Rights Reserved. Poetry Archives is a wholly owned subsidy of eMule.com.)

A commercial site that offers access to thousands of copyright-free
poems--that is, primarily poems written before 1920.

Literary History.com: Indexing the Internet
(Copyright © 1998-2004 by Jan Pridmore.)

Links to articles on particular authors and genres, from the nineteenth and twentieth centuries. The "20th Century Poetry" section provides a good overview of major authors and schools of poetry (borrowed, as the web-author notes, from David Perkins's A History of Modern Poetry.)

Modern American Poetry: An Online Journal and Multimedia Companion to Anthology of Modern American Poetry (Oxford University Press, 2000) Edited by Cary Nelson

(Copyright © 1999, 2000, 2001, 2002 by the Department of English, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. This site, and all information herein, is intended for non-profit, educational use only.)

"MAPS is a comprehensive learning environment and scholarly forum for the study of modern American poetry." Includes biographies of poets and their own commentaries, and those of critics and other poets, on their work.

Dia Art Foundation
(Copyright © 2004 Dia Art Foundation)

["Dia Art Foundation, founded in 1974, is a nonprofit institution that plays a vital role among visual arts organizations nationally and internationally by initiating, supporting, presenting, and preserving art projects, and by serving as a locus for interdisciplinary art and criticism."]

In addition to exhibition catalogues, artist web projects, and links to their bookshop, see Dia's poetry section (under "Programs" in the "Chelsea" heading) that features texts (and some audio recordings) by poets who have read at the center, including John Ashbery, Wole Soyinka, Sharon Olds, Galway Kinnell, Quincy Troupe, and many others.

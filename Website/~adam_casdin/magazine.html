Online Literary Magazines

Jacket Magazine

(Jacket is edited by John Tranter, published from Syndney, Australia, and sponsored by Australian Literary Management.)

As the site says, "Jacket is a free Internet-only quarterly review of new writing, with poetry, creative prose, interviews, reviews, and informative feature articles."

Timothy McSweeney's


An offshoot of McSweeney's, the quarterly print publication. Features short fiction and expository writing.

The Cortland Review: An Online Literary Magazine in RealAudio


(Edited by Guy Shahar. Founded in 1997.) A quarterly on-line journal that publishes new and unpublished material. Many features are available in RealAudio. As stated on the site, "The Cortland Review publishes poetry, fiction, essays, reviews. Our loyalty is to the aesthetic of poetry itself, not a particular school or discipline. We venture to publish the very best material we receive, regardless of form or content."

Rain Taxi

: Review of Books.
(Edited by Eric Loberer and based in Minneapolis.)
"Rain Taxi, a winner of the Alternative Press Award for Best Arts & Literature Coverage, is a quarterly publication that publishes reviews of literary fiction, poetry, and nonfiction." The publication also includes author interviews and the occasional article. The print edition is separate and offers different articles.

Narrativity

: A Crtical Journal of Innovative Narrative.
(Published out of San Francisco State University. Edited by Mary Burger, Robert Gluck, Camille Roy, and Gail Scott)
Here's their mini-manifesto: "We intend for Narrativity to supply a form of nutrition now missing: the articulation of ideas about theory-based narrative. We hope to provide a meeting place in which writers develop a critical discourse on uses of narrative in their own and others' work. Above all, we want you, your participation, your feedback."

Milk Magazine

(Created in 1999 by Larry Sawyer and Lina ramona Vitkauskas.)

As stated on the site, "[w]ith milkmag.org [Sawyer] hopes to make contact with the spirit of poetry via the electronic pulse of this strange contraption called the Internet, infusing it with a life it lacks, the creative sweat and blood of the inspiring artists contained herein."

Litline: A Website for the Independent Literary Community

Litline, published out of Illinois State University, Normal, offers the most extensive list of online magazines I have found.
